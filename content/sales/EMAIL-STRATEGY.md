# Email strategy (read this first)

## Send FROM Logo Clothing Shop

**Yes. Stick with your Logo Clothing Shop email for now.**

Your clients know you. They have your number. They do not know Stitch and Stone yet. Sending from a new domain feels like a stranger showed up.

| Use now | Save for launch |
|---------|-----------------|
| **From:** hello@logoclothingshop.com | hello@stitchandstoneco.com |
| **Signature:** Logo Clothing Shop | Lead with Stitch and Stone |
| **Subject lines:** your name or their problem | New brand in subject |

**Confirmed send address:** hello@logoclothingshop.com (website, email, marketing — monitored daily).

---

## Stitch and Stone = subtle introduction only

One quiet mention in the email body. Not in the subject. Not in the From line. Not the headline.

Example line (use once):

> Fair warning, we're rolling out a new name eventually, Stitch and Stone Co. Same team, same shop. You'll still know us as Logo Clothing Shop.

The one-pager can keep the small bridge line under the logo. That's enough.

---

## Sandler rules for these emails

1. **Lead with their world, not your brochure.** Pain first. Product second.
2. **Permission and upfront contract.** "Not sure this is even on your plate." "Totally fine if the timing's off."
3. **Mutual qualification.** You're deciding if they're a fit too. Not chasing.
4. **One soft CTA.** Book a call to see if it makes sense. Or reply. Their choice.
5. **Short.** Write it like you talk into the mic. If you wouldn't say it out loud, cut it.
6. **No em dashes.** Ever. Use periods, commas, or start a new sentence.
7. **Stories, not numbered pitch decks.** One or two examples woven in. Save the third for the PDF.
